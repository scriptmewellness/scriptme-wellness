Pillar Three

Physical Health

Your physical health is the foundation everything else is built upon — from blood sugar and insulin balance, to hormones, heart health, and cognitive function, every system in your body is deeply interconnected. We take a proactive, root-cause approach to chronic disease prevention and management, working to optimize your body from the inside out so you can feel your best at every stage of life.

01

Connected, not compartmentalized

Conventional medicine treats organs in isolation. Cardiology over here, endocrinology over there, gastroenterology somewhere else. But your thyroid, your gut, your hormones, and your heart all communicate constantly. We treat the system, not just the symptom.

02

Prevention before damage

Most chronic disease is preventable, but only if you catch it years before it shows up on a standard panel. We look at the early signals — fasting insulin, inflammation markers, advanced lipid panels — that tell us what's happening before it becomes irreversible.

03

Optimal, not just normal

Lab "normal" ranges are based on the average — and the average American is not well. We work with optimal ranges, the levels that actually correspond to feeling and functioning your best. The difference shows up in how you feel.

Going Deeper

What "root cause" really means in functional medicine

Every conventional doctor says they treat root causes. Functional medicine actually does. Here's what the difference looks like in practice.

Standard lab ranges were never designed to tell you whether you're healthy. They were designed to flag obvious disease. The reference range for TSH, for example, runs from roughly 0.4 to 4.5 — but the patients who feel best generally fall between 1.0 and 2.0. Anyone in the upper half of "normal" can be quietly suffering with the lab telling them everything is fine.

This pattern repeats across nearly every test. Vitamin D, B12, ferritin, fasting insulin, hsCRP — the gap between "not yet diseased" and "thriving" is huge, and conventional medicine often doesn't acknowledge it exists.

We look for the optimal range — where the literature says people actually function their best — and we treat the gap between where you are and where you could be.

A typical annual physical includes a basic metabolic panel, lipid panel, and TSH. It's a fine starting point, but it misses most of what matters for prevention.

What we add to a comprehensive workup:

  • Fasting insulin — catches insulin resistance years before glucose rises
  • Hemoglobin A1c with fasting glucose — together, they tell a much better story than either alone
  • Full thyroid panel — TSH, free T3, free T4, reverse T3, antibodies
  • Advanced lipid panel — LDL particle number and size, ApoB, Lp(a) — the markers that actually predict cardiac events
  • hsCRP and homocysteine — inflammation and cardiovascular markers
  • Comprehensive nutrient testing — D, B12, folate, magnesium, iron studies, ferritin
  • Sex hormones — total and free testosterone, estradiol, progesterone, DHEA-S
  • Cortisol rhythm — when indicated, four-point salivary or urinary panels

The goal isn't to run every test that exists. It's to run the right ones for your situation, and to actually interpret them in context.

Type 2 diabetes doesn't appear overnight. By the time fasting glucose rises into the diabetic range, insulin resistance has typically been present for 10-15 years. The same pattern holds for cardiovascular disease, autoimmune conditions, and many cancers.

This is the most important thing we can offer in functional medicine: the chance to catch the trajectory before it becomes the destination. The work isn't dramatic. It's targeted nutrition, smart exercise, sleep, stress management, and sometimes specific supplementation — all directed at the early signals that conventional medicine ignores.

If you have a family history of cardiovascular disease, diabetes, autoimmune conditions, or cancer, this is the time to act. Not when symptoms appear.

Real change in physical health takes time and attention. We don't believe in quarterly check-ins where nothing of substance happens. Working with us looks like:

  • A comprehensive initial workup — full labs, full history, full hour with Emily
  • A written, prioritized plan with the "why" behind every recommendation
  • Targeted follow-up testing at the right intervals to track progress
  • Adjustments as your body responds — not waiting six months to course-correct
  • Coordination with your specialists when needed — we work alongside your other doctors, not instead of them

The goal is for your physical health to become something you understand and feel ownership of, not a black box managed by other people.
